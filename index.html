Chào mừng! Welcome! Here's how to get started:

This app is designed specifically for Vietnamese learners (Người Việt học tiếng Anh). It has everything you need: pronunciation guide with Vietnamese comparisons, 1000 essential words with translations, and grammar with Vietnamese explanations.

Daily Study Plan (Kế hoạch học mỗi ngày)

Research shows short daily sessions work best. Try 2-3 blocks of 15-20 minutes:

  • Morning (Sáng) — 15 min: Learn 5 new words + review 5 old words using flashcards
  • Afternoon (Chiều) — 15 min: Read one grammar section, practice making sentences
  • Evening (Tối) — 10 min: Take the quiz, listen to pronunciation of today's words

Consistency beats intensity! 15 minutes every day > 2 hours on Sunday. (Học đều đặn mỗi ngày quan trọng hơn học dồn!)

  1. Start with IPA Pronunciation (Bắt đầu với phát âm) — Learn the sound symbols first. Pay special attention to sounds that don't exist in Vietnamese: /θ/ (think), /ð/ (this), ending consonants. Click speaker icons to hear each sound.
  2. Set your daily target (Đặt mục tiêu mỗi ngày) — Start with 10 words/day. Use the "Today's Words" button for a mix of new + review words.
  3. Learn vocabulary by topic (Học từ vựng theo chủ đề) — Start with "Greetings", then "Numbers". Mark words as "Learned" when you can pronounce them AND know the meaning.
  4. Test yourself (Tự kiểm tra) — Use Flashcards to review, then Quiz to test. Aim for 80%+ before moving to the next topic.
  5. Study grammar alongside (Học ngữ pháp song song) — After 50-100 words, start the grammar section. Vietnamese translations explain each tense.
  6. Use ChatGPT to practice (Dùng ChatGPT để luyện tập) — See the "ChatGPT Practice Guide" section below for ready-to-use prompts!

Common Mistakes for Vietnamese Learners

Những lỗi phổ biến của người Việt khi học tiếng Anh — and how to fix them!

1. Dropping Ending Consonants (Nuốt phụ âm cuối)

Vietnamese words don't end with consonant sounds, so Vietnamese learners often skip them in English. This is the #1 mistake!

Tiếng Việt không có phụ âm cuối, nên người Việt hay "nuốt" âm cuối khi nói tiếng Anh.

nigh → night /naɪt/ — Don't drop the /t/!
rai → right /raɪt/ — The /t/ at the end matters!
mou → mouse /maʊs/ — The /s/ changes the meaning!
mana → manage /ˈmæn.ɪdʒ/ — Pronounce the /dʒ/ ending!
💡 Practice Tip

Exaggerate ending sounds when practicing. Say "night-TUH", "mouse-SUH" at first, then gradually soften. The ending should be short but audible.

Mẹo: Phóng đại âm cuối khi luyện tập. Nói "night-TỪ" trước, rồi dần dần nhẹ đi.

2. Confusing /l/ and /r/ (Nhầm lẫn âm /l/ và /r/)

Vietnamese /l/ and /r/ are very different from English. Vietnamese speakers often say /n/ instead of /l/, and /z/ instead of /r/.

/l/ tiếng Anh: đầu lưỡi chạm lợi trên, giữ nguyên. /r/ tiếng Anh: uốn lưỡi ra sau, KHÔNG chạm đâu cả.

"rice" sounds like "lice" → Practice: /r/ = curl tongue back, /l/ = tongue tip touches ridge
"right" sounds like "light" → These are DIFFERENT words with DIFFERENT meanings!
"never" → "nevơ" → Say: /ˈnev.ər/ — keep the /r/ even in the middle!
💡 Practice Tip

For /r/: pretend you're a dog growling "rrrr" — your tongue should NOT touch anything. For /l/: your tongue tip firmly touches the ridge behind your upper teeth.

3. The /θ/ and /ð/ Sounds (Âm "th" — không có trong tiếng Việt!)

These two sounds don't exist in Vietnamese at all! Vietnamese speakers replace /θ/ with /t/ and /ð/ with /d/ or /z/.

Cách phát âm: Đặt đầu lưỡi giữa hai hàm răng, thổi hơi qua. /θ/ = không rung, /ð/ = rung dây thanh.

"think" → "tink" → /θɪŋk/ — tongue between teeth!
"this" → "dis" → /ðɪs/ — tongue between teeth + voice!
"three" → "tree" → /θriː/ — "three" ≠ "tree"!
"weather" → "wedder" → /ˈweð.ər/ — tongue between teeth!
💡 Practice Tip

Put your finger in front of your mouth. Say "think" — you should feel air on your finger through the gap between tongue and teeth. If you don't feel air, your tongue isn't far enough forward.

4. Dropping "to be" (Quên động từ "to be")

In Vietnamese, you say "Nó đói" (He hungry). In English, you MUST include "is/am/are": "He is hungry."

Tiếng Việt không cần "là" trước tính từ, nhưng tiếng Anh BẮT BUỘC phải có "is/am/are".

She beautiful. → She is beautiful.
I hungry. → I am hungry.
They happy. → They are happy.
The food delicious. → The food is delicious.
💡 Practice Tip

Rule: Subject + is/am/are + adjective. ALWAYS. No exceptions. When you describe something/someone, ask yourself: "Did I include is/am/are?"

5. Wrong Word Stress & Flat Intonation (Sai trọng âm & nói đều đều)

Vietnamese is tonal (each syllable has a tone), but English uses word stress (one syllable is louder/longer). Vietnamese speakers often make every syllable equal, which sounds robotic.

Tiếng Anh nhấn mạnh MỘT âm tiết trong từ. Ví dụ: baNAna (nhấn "na" giữa), COMputer (nhấn "com").

com-PU-ter (all equal) → com-PU-ter (stress the middle)
ba-na-na (all equal) → ba-NA-na (stress the second)
im-por-tant (all equal) → im-POR-tant (stress the middle)
💡 Practice Tip

When learning a new word, always learn WHERE the stress falls. Clap your hands on the stressed syllable. Wrong stress can make you completely misunderstood!

ChatGPT Practice Guide

Use ChatGPT as your free English tutor — here are ready-to-use prompts for each stage of learning. (Dùng ChatGPT như gia sư miễn phí!)

📌 When to Use This Site vs ChatGPT

Use This Site For:
  • Learning IPA pronunciation
  • Building your first 1000 words
  • Understanding grammar rules
  • Daily target tracking
  • Flashcards & quizzes
  • Vietnamese explanations
Use ChatGPT For:
  • Practicing conversations
  • Getting sentence corrections
  • Asking "why" questions
  • Role-play scenarios
  • Writing practice
  • Hearing pronunciation (voice mode)

🟢 Stage 1: First 100 Words (Giai đoạn 1: 100 từ đầu tiên)

Use these prompts AFTER learning words on this site. Copy and paste into ChatGPT:

Prompt 1 — Check my pronunciation understanding:
I'm a Vietnamese beginner learning English. I just learned these words: [paste 5-10 words]. Can you make simple sentences using each word and explain the pronunciation that Vietnamese speakers usually get wrong? Please explain in both English and Vietnamese.
Prompt 2 — Simple conversation practice:
Act as a friendly English teacher for a Vietnamese beginner. Let's have a very simple conversation using only basic words (greetings, numbers, family). Use short sentences (max 5-6 words). After each of my replies, gently correct any mistakes and explain in Vietnamese why. Start the conversation by greeting me.
Prompt 3 — Word connection game:
Let's play a word game. I'll say an English word, and you tell me: 1) A simple sentence using it, 2) One word that is related to it, 3) The Vietnamese translation. Keep everything very simple for a beginner. Start with the word "hello".

🟡 Stage 2: 100-500 Words (Giai đoạn 2: Đã biết 100-500 từ)

Now you can have real conversations and practice grammar:

Prompt 4 — Grammar practice:
I'm a Vietnamese learner studying English grammar. Today I want to practice [Present Simple / Present Continuous / Past Simple]. Give me 5 fill-in-the-blank exercises, then check my answers. Explain mistakes in Vietnamese. Focus on mistakes that Vietnamese speakers commonly make (like forgetting "is/am/are" or dropping -s/-es).
Prompt 5 — Daily life role-play:
Let's role-play! You are a [waiter at a restaurant / hotel receptionist / shop assistant / doctor]. I am the customer/patient. Have a realistic conversation with me. Use simple English. After the conversation, list the new vocabulary I should learn and give me the Vietnamese translations.
Prompt 6 — Fix my sentences:
I'm going to write 5 sentences in English. Please: 1) Tell me if each sentence is correct or incorrect, 2) Fix any mistakes, 3) Explain WHY it's wrong in simple English and Vietnamese, 4) Give me a better way to say it. Here are my sentences: [write your sentences]

🔵 Stage 3: 500+ Words (Giai đoạn 3: Đã biết 500+ từ)

You're getting stronger! Practice more complex conversations:

Prompt 7 — IT/Work English:
I'm a Vietnamese person working in IT. Let's practice a work conversation in English. Simulate a [daily standup meeting / code review discussion / email to my manager about a deadline / Slack message to a teammate]. Use real IT vocabulary. After the practice, list key phrases I should memorize.
Prompt 8 — Email writing:
I need to write a professional email in English for this situation: [describe your situation in Vietnamese or English]. Please: 1) Write the email for me, 2) Explain the key phrases, 3) Give me a template I can reuse, 4) Show me common mistakes Vietnamese speakers make in emails.
Prompt 9 — Debate & opinion practice:
Let's practice expressing opinions in English. The topic is: [remote work / AI in education / living in the city vs countryside]. Ask me questions, let me answer, then help me express my ideas more naturally. Correct my grammar and suggest better vocabulary. Explain in Vietnamese when needed.

🎤 Bonus: Voice Practice with ChatGPT App

ChatGPT mobile app has a voice mode! Tap the headphone icon to talk instead of type. This is the best way to practice speaking when you don't have an English-speaking friend.

Ứng dụng ChatGPT có chế độ nói chuyện bằng giọng nói! Nhấn biểu tượng tai nghe để nói thay vì gõ. Đây là cách tốt nhất để luyện nói khi bạn không có bạn nói tiếng Anh.

Voice practice prompt:
I'm a Vietnamese beginner. Let's have a slow, simple English conversation. Speak clearly and slowly. If I make a mistake, gently correct me and repeat the correct version. Let's talk about [my daily routine / my favorite food / my job].
💡 Important Tips (Mẹo quan trọng)

1. Always tell ChatGPT you are Vietnamese — it will focus on Vietnamese-specific mistakes.

2. Ask ChatGPT to explain in Vietnamese when you don't understand something.

3. Don't just read ChatGPT's replies — say them out loud! (Đừng chỉ đọc thầm — hãy nói to lên!)

4. Practice 10-15 minutes with ChatGPT AFTER studying on this site, not instead of it.

5. Save conversations you found useful — you can review them later.
